Uptown Funk Copyright Infringement Case Summary:



"Uptown Funk" is a song by British record producer Mark Ronson, featuring American singer and songwriter Bruno Mars. It was released on 10 November 2014, as the lead single from Ronson's fourth studio album, Uptown Special (2015).

Written by Mark Ronson, Bruno Mars, Philip Lawrence, Jeff Bhasker, Nicholas Williams, Devon Gallaspy, Charles Wilson, Robert Wilson, Ronnie Wilson, Rudolph Taylor, Lonnie Simmons.


Album Information

  • Album: Uptown Special
  • Released: 10 November 2014
  • Artist: Mark Ronson
  • Featured artist: Bruno Mars
  • Genre: Pop
  • Label: Columbia, Sony, RCA

Various artists and songwriters alleged that "Uptown Funk" copied elements from their earlier compositions, including "Funk You Up" by The Sequence and "Oops Up Side Your Head" by The Gap Band. They claimed that "Uptown Funk" infringed upon their copyright by using similar rhythms, melodies, and lyrics.


The lawsuit was filed in 2016, with The Sequence claiming that "Uptown Funk" copied their 1979 song "Funk You Up." The Gap Band's publishers also joined the lawsuit, alleging similarities between "Uptown Funk" and their 1979 track "Oops Up Side Your Head."

The plaintiffs argued that "Uptown Funk" had appropriated significant portions of their songs without permission.
